Alan Mathison Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London, England.

  • 1927 - Shows remarkable ability solving advanced problems in 1927 without having studied even elementary calculus.

  • 1936 - Publishes his paper "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem" (Turing Machine), in the Proceedings of the London Mathematical Society journal.

  • 1938 - Obtains his PhD from the Department of Mathematics at Princeton; his dissertation, Systems of Logic Based on Ordinals.

  • 1939 - In september, the day after the UK declared war on Germany, Turing was reported to Bletchley Park, the wartime station of GC&CS.

    • 1939 - In december solves the essential part of the German naval indicator system, which was more complex than the indicator systems used by the other services.

  • 1942 - Travels to the United States and works with US Navy cryptanalysts on the naval Enigma and bombe construction in Washington.

  • 1946 - Presents a paper which was the first detailed design of a stored-program computer.

  • 1950 - Addresses the problem of artificial intelligence, and proposes an experiment that became known as the Turing test, an attempt to define a standard for a machine to be called "intelligent".

  • 1952 - Convicted on "gross indecency" because his homosexuallity and given a choice between imprisonment and probation.

  • 1954 - Dies at the age of 41 by suicide with poison.

"So on behalf of the British government, and all those who live freely thanks to Alan's work I am very proud to say: we're sorry, you deserved so much better."

British Prime Minister, Gordon Brown (10 September 2009)
